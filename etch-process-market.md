<p><p>Etch process market types refer to the different methods used for removing material from a substrate to create patterns or shapes in semiconductor manufacturing. Dry etching involves using plasma or ions to etch the material, offering high precision and uniformity, making it suitable for advanced applications. Wet etching, on the other hand, involves using chemical solutions to etch the material, providing simple and cost-effective processing, but with less precision. Both markets cater to the semiconductor industry, with dry etching being popular in high-tech applications and wet etching being more commonly used in conventional manufacturing.</p></p>
