<p><ul><li>Ocean Thermal Energy</li><li>Mechanical Energy</li><li>Ocean Salinity Energy</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The ocean energy market encompasses various types of energy sources. Ocean Thermal Energy refers to the utilization of temperature differences between the warm surface water and the colder deep ocean water to generate electricity. Mechanical Energy involves harnessing the power of ocean waves and currents to produce electricity. Ocean Salinity Energy focuses on extracting energy from the difference in salt concentration between seawater and fresh water. Other types include tidal energy, which involves generating electricity from the natural ebb and flow of tides, and osmotic energy, which utilizes the power generated from the movement of saltwater and freshwater through a membrane.</p></p>
