<p><p>Viscosity sensors are used in various industries to measure the thickness or resistance to flow of liquids. Sonic sensors use sound waves to analyze the viscosity of a liquid, emitting a sound wave and measuring the time it takes to travel through the liquid. Mechanical sensors, on the other hand, rely on physical movement or rotation of a probe to measure the liquid's viscosity. Both types of sensors provide accurate viscosity readings for industries such as chemicals, pharmaceuticals, and food processing, aiding in quality control and process optimization.</p></p>
