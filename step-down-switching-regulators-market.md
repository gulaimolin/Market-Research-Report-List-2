<p><p>Step down switching regulators, also known as buck converters or buck controllers, are commonly used in electronic devices to convert a higher input voltage to a lower output voltage. The buck converter is a type of step down regulator that uses an inductor and a diode to achieve voltage reduction. On the other hand, the buck controller utilizes a pulse width modulation (PWM) control scheme to regulate the output voltage. Both types of regulators are vital in maintaining stable and efficient power supply for various applications, such as battery chargers, mobile devices, and automotive electronics.</p></p>
