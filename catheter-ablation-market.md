<p><p>Catheter ablation is a minimally invasive procedure used to treat cardiac arrhythmias by destroying abnormal heart tissues that cause irregular heart rhythms. It involves inserting a catheter (a thin, flexible tube) through a blood vessel in the groin or arm and threading it to the specific area of the heart causing the arrhythmia. Once in position, the catheter emits radiofrequency energy or extreme cold to destroy the targeted cells and restore a normal heart rhythm.</p><p>The catheter ablation market has witnessed significant growth in recent years due to factors like increasing prevalence of cardiac arrhythmias, advancements in ablation technologies, and rising adoption of minimally invasive procedures. Additionally, the aging population and the growing demand for improved healthcare facilities worldwide are further driving market growth.</p><p>The market is also witnessing several trends that are shaping its growth trajectory.
