<p><p>IV tube, also known as intravenous tube or infusion tubing, is a medical device used for the delivery of fluids, such as medication, nutrients, or blood, directly into the veins of a patient. It consists of a long plastic tube with a needle or catheter at one end and a connector at the other end to attach to an IV bag or syringe. IV tubes are widely used in hospitals, clinics, and home care settings.</p><p>The IV tube market is experiencing significant growth and is expected to continue expanding at a CAGR of 12.7% during the forecast period. The increasing prevalence of chronic diseases, such as cancer and diabetes, is one of the primary factors driving market growth. IV tubes are commonly used for the administration of chemotherapy drugs and insulin therapy, contributing to the rising demand.</p><p>Advancements in technology have also played a crucial role in the growth of the IV tube market. Manufacturers are focusing on the development of innovative IV tube designs that improve safety, reduce the risk of infections, and enhance patient comfort.
