<p><p>The Opioid Induced Constipation (OIC) drug market features various types of drugs to address this condition. Lubiprostone, a selective chloride channel activator, helps increase fluid secretion in the intestine, easing bowel movements. Methyl Naltrexone Bromide is an antagonist that prevents opioids from binding to receptors in the gut, enabling regular bowel movements. Naldemedine, another antagonist, reduces constipation by blocking opioid activity in the gut. Alvimopan, a peripherally acting mu-opioid receptor antagonist, also promotes normal bowel function. Additionally, there are other drugs available to treat OIC, each with unique mechanisms aimed at alleviating opioid-induced constipation.</p></p>
