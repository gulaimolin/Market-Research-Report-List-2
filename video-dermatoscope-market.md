<p><p>Video dermatoscopes are handheld devices used by dermatologists to examine skin lesions. There are two main types of video dermatoscopes: traditional and digital. Traditional dermatoscopes use magnifying lenses and a light source to evaluate skin conditions. They require the use of the human eye to view and interpret the images. In contrast, digital dermatoscopes capture high-resolution images and display them on a screen, allowing for easier visualization and analysis. They also offer features like image storage and sharing, enhancing dermatologists' ability to diagnose and monitor skin conditions.</p></p>
