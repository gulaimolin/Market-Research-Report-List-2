<p><p>Venous thromboembolism (VTE) therapy refers to the medical treatment aimed at preventing or treating blood clots that occur in veins, particularly deep vein thrombosis (DVT) and pulmonary embolism (PE). These conditions are associated with high morbidity and mortality rates and require prompt diagnosis and intervention. VTE therapy generally involves the use of anticoagulant medications, compression stockings, and in severe cases, surgical interventions.</p><p>The market for VTE therapy has experienced significant growth in recent years, and it is expected to continue its upward trajectory during the forecast period. The increasing prevalence of VTE, owing to factors like obesity, sedentary lifestyle, aging population, and the rising incidence of chronic diseases, is a primary driver of market growth. Additionally, the growing awareness regarding the importance of early diagnosis and treatment and advancements in drug therapies are bolstering the demand for VTE therapy.</p><p>Moreover, with the increasing number of surgical procedures and hospitalizations, the risk of VTE also rises, further propelling market growth. The development of novel oral anticoagulants (NOACs) that provide better therapeutic options and have fewer side effects compared to traditional therapies has also contributed to the market's expansion.</p><p>In terms of trends, there has been a shift towards outpatient settings for the management of VTE, reducing hospital stays and healthcare costs.
