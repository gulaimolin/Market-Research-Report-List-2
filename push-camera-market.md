<p><p>A push camera, also known as a pipe inspection camera, is a type of portable video camera system designed to inspect pipes, ducts, and other narrow spaces. These cameras are typically mounted on a flexible rod or cable, allowing them to be pushed or maneuvered into tight spaces for visual inspection.</p><p>The push camera market has witnessed significant growth in recent years, primarily driven by the increasing demand for sewer inspection and maintenance activities in both residential and industrial sectors. The rapid urbanization and infrastructure development have led to the expansion of sewer networks, creating a need for regular inspection to detect any damages or blockages. Push cameras are a cost-effective solution for conducting such inspections as they eliminate the need for extensive excavation work.</p><p>Moreover, the advancements in camera technology, such as high-definition imaging and wireless connectivity options, have further fueled the market growth. These features enable real-time visualization and recording of inspections, improving the efficiency and accuracy of the inspection process.</p><p>Furthermore, the push camera market has witnessed a surge in demand from various industries, including oil and gas, manufacturing, construction, and municipal utilities.
