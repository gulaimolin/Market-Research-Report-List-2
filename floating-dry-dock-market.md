<p><p>A floating dry dock is a type of large structure that is used to lift ships out of the water for maintenance and repairs. It consists of a watertight basin that can be flooded to allow a vessel to float in, and then pumped out to lift the vessel out of the water. This allows for convenient access to the ship's hull, propeller, and other underwater parts that need to be inspected or repaired.</p><p>The global floating dry dock market is expected to experience significant growth in the forecast period. The increasing focus on ship repair and maintenance activities, especially in emerging economies, is driving the market. Additionally, the growing demand for commercial vessels, such as container ships and oil tankers, is also fueling the market growth.</p><p>The market is also witnessing several trends that are shaping its growth. One of the major trends is the development of advanced floating dry docks with enhanced capabilities and efficiency. These docks are equipped with modern technology and automation, allowing for faster and more accurate ship repair and maintenance processes. Moreover, there is a rising demand for larger floating dry docks that can accommodate bigger vessels.</p><p>Furthermore, environmental concerns are also driving the market. Floating dry docks are considered more environmentally friendly as they eliminate the need for traditional dry docks that involve land reclamation. This is leading to a growing preference for floating dry docks, especially in regions with strict environmental regulations.</p><p>To conclude, the floating dry dock market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 6.8% during the forecast period.
