<p><p>In-Vehicle Networking refers to the communication systems used within vehicles to connect various electronic components. The main types of In-Vehicle Networking are CAN (Controller Area Network), LIN (Local Interconnect Network), FlexRay, and Ethernet. CAN is the most widely adopted, offering reliable and cost-effective communication between different vehicle systems. LIN is a simpler and more affordable option, commonly used for less critical functions. FlexRay provides high-speed and fault-tolerant communication for advanced safety systems. Ethernet is gaining popularity for its higher bandwidth capabilities, supporting the increasing data requirements of modern vehicles.</p></p>
