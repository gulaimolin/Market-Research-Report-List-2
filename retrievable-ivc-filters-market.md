<p><p>Retrievable IVC filters are small, cage-like devices that are implanted in the inferior vena cava (IVC) to catch blood clots and prevent them from travelling to the lungs. These filters are considered retrievable because they can be removed once the risk of blood clot formation has passed. The demand for retrievable IVC filters is increasing due to the rise in venous thromboembolism (VTE) cases worldwide.</p><p>The current outlook of the retrievable IVC filters market is positive and expected to grow at a CAGR of 7.6% during the forecasted period. Factors driving market growth include an increase in the aging population, a rise in lifestyle-related diseases, and advancements in IVC filter technology. Additionally, the increasing awareness among healthcare professionals and patients about the benefits of retrievable IVC filters is boosting market growth.</p><p>The market forecast for retrievable IVC filters is promising, with a significant growth potential in the coming years. The market is expected to witness a rise in demand due to the high prevalence of deep vein thrombosis (DVT) and pulmonary embolism (PE). Moreover, technological advancements in retrievable IVC filters, such as the use of bioabsorbable materials and improved filter designs, are likely to drive market growth.</p><p>In terms of market trends, there is a growing preference for retrievable filters over permanent filters, as they provide better long-term outcomes and reduce the risks associated with permanent implantation. Additionally, the development of novel retrieval techniques and the introduction of next-generation IVC filters are expected to dominate the market in the future.</p><p>Overall, the retrievable IVC filters market is poised for substantial growth in the coming years, driven by factors such as increasing patient awareness, technological advancements, and rising healthcare expenditure.</p></p>

<p><p>The Retrievable IVC Filters market includes two main types, namely the Tethered Filter and the Unrestricted-type Filter. Tethered Filters are designed with a mechanism that allows them to be easily retrieved by medical professionals when necessary. On the other hand, Unrestricted-type Filters do not have this retrieval mechanism and are left permanently in place. Both types aim to prevent blood clots from traveling to the lungs, but Tethered Filters offer the advantage of easier removal when needed.</p></p>
