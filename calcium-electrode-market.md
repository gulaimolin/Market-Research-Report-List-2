<p><p>A calcium electrode is a device used in electrochemical systems to measure the concentration of calcium ions in a solution. It consists of a calcium-sensitive membrane and an internal reference electrode, which generate an electrical potential proportional to the calcium ion activity in the solution. This measurement can be used in a variety of applications, such as monitoring calcium levels in blood, water, or food processing.</p><p>The current outlook of the calcium electrode market is positive, and it is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years.
