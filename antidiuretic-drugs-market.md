<p><p>Antidiuretic drugs, also known as ADH (antidiuretic hormone) agonists, are pharmaceuticals that help regulate water balance in the body by reducing urine production. Vasopressin, a natural hormone, and its synthetic form, desmopressin, are commonly used antidiuretic drugs. They work by increasing the absorption of water in the kidneys. Oxytocin, another hormone, is also considered an antidiuretic drug as it decreases urine output by constricting blood vessels. "Others" in this context refer to alternative antidiuretic drugs available in the market, which may have different mechanisms of action or less common usage.</p></p>
