<p><strong>The Wood Vinegar Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Slow Pyrolysis</li><li>Fast Pyrolysis</li><li>Intermediate Pyrolysis</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Wood vinegar, a byproduct of wood pyrolysis, has three different market types based on the pyrolysis process used. Slow pyrolysis involves heating wood at a low temperature for a longer time, resulting in a high yield of wood vinegar. Fast pyrolysis, on the other hand, uses high temperatures for a short time, producing a low yield of wood vinegar but yielding more biochar and bio-oil. Intermediate pyrolysis falls between slow and fast pyrolysis in terms of both temperature and time, offering a balanced production of wood vinegar, bio-oil, and biochar.</p></p>
