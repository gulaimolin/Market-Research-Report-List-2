<p><p>A semiconductor laser diode is a device that produces coherent light by converting electrical energy into laser radiation through a process called stimulated emission. It is made up of a p-n junction diode, where the junction is formed by two types of semiconductor materials, p-type and n-type. When a current is applied to the diode, it excites the electrons and releases photons, resulting in laser emission.</p><p>The current outlook of the semiconductor laser diode market is highly promising. With the increasing demand for high-speed internet connectivity, the need for laser diodes in telecommunication applications is rising significantly.
