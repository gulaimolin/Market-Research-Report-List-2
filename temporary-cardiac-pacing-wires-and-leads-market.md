<p><p>Temporary cardiac pacing wires and leads are used to manage and treat heart arrhythmias in emergency situations. Unipolar pacing wires consist of one electrode that is attached to the heart, while the other end is connected to an external pulse generator. Bipolar pacing wires have two electrodes, one of which is placed inside the heart and the other outside. Other types may include dual chamber pacing wires, which have two leads for pacing both the atria and ventricles. These different market types offer varying levels of therapeutic benefits and have specific applications based on the patient's condition and the preferred pacing strategy.</p></p>
