<p><p>3D bioprinting refers to the process of creating three-dimensional biological structures using specialized printers. The magnetic 3D bioprinting market involves the use of magnetic fields to precisely position and arrange cells or bioinks. Laser-assisted bioprinting utilizes laser beams to focus and deposit cells or biomaterials layer by layer. Inkjet 3D bioprinting is based on the inkjet printing technology, where bioinks are ejected through a printhead nozzle. Microextrusion 3D bioprinting employs a microscale nozzle to extrude bioinks or cells, enabling the precise deposition of materials for the formation of complex tissues or organs.</p></p>
