<p><p>Aircraft brakes are an essential component of an aircraft's landing system, responsible for enabling the aircraft to stop or slow down during landing or taxiing. These brakes are typically located in the main landing gear and function by converting kinetic energy into thermal energy through friction. Aircraft brakes must be highly reliable, durable, and efficient to withstand the high temperatures and pressures generated during braking.</p><p>The aircraft brakes market has been exhibiting a steady growth trajectory in recent years and is expected to continue this trend during the forecast period. The market growth is primarily driven by the increasing commercial air travel and the subsequent rise in demand for new aircraft. Additionally, advancements in brake technologies to enhance efficiency and safety are also contributing to market growth.</p><p>One of the prominent trends in the aircraft brakes market is the increasing adoption of carbon brakes. Carbon brakes offer several advantages over traditional steel brakes, including reduced weight, higher durability, and better heat dissipation. These benefits make carbon brakes ideal for modern aircraft designs, contributing to their growing popularity in the market.</p><p>Another trend seen in the market is the integration of braking systems with aircraft telemetry systems. This integration allows for real-time monitoring and analysis of brake performance, enabling proactive maintenance and reducing the risk of failures or accidents.</p><p>Overall, the aircraft brakes market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. The increasing demand for new aircraft, advancements in brake technologies, and the adoption of carbon brakes are the key factors driving this growth. The market is anticipated to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 13.5% during the forecast period.</p></p>
