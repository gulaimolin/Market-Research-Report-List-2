<p><p>The Smart Grid refers to an advanced electrical grid system that employs digital communication technology to enhance the efficiency, reliability, and sustainability of electricity delivery. Smart grid technologies provide enhanced monitoring, control, and optimization capabilities, ensuring a reliable power supply to meet the growing energy demands.</p><p>Furthermore, governments worldwide are focusing on the integration of renewable energy sources into the grid to reduce carbon emissions and achieve sustainability goals. Smart grids enable the integration of renewable energy sources such as solar and wind power and facilitate effective management and distribution of this energy. This factor is expected to propel the growth of the smart grid market.</p><p>In addition, the increasing deployment of electric vehicles (EVs) is creating the need for a sophisticated charging infrastructure. Smart grid technologies enable intelligent EV charging, optimizing the charging patterns and minimizing strain on the grid during peak demand periods.
