<p><ul><li>Cytopathological Method</li><li>Molecular Method</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Cervical cancer diagnostic testing devices are classified into two types: cytopathological method and molecular method. The cytopathological method involves analyzing cells collected from the cervix under a microscope to check for any abnormal changes that may indicate cancer. On the other hand, the molecular method uses DNA-based tests to detect the presence of HPV (human papillomavirus) or genetic mutations associated with cervical cancer. Both methods are used to accurately diagnose cervical cancer and determine appropriate treatment options.</p></p>
