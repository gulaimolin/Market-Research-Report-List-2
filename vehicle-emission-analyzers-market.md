<p><strong>The Vehicle Emission Analyzers Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Non-dispersive Infrared (NDIR) Analyzers</li><li>Flame Ionization Detector (FID)</li><li>Chemiluminescence Analyzer (CLA)</li><li>Constant Volume Sampler (CVS)</li><li>Other Type</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Vehicle emission analyzers are used to measure and analyze the exhaust gases emitted by vehicles for regulatory and environmental purposes. There are several types of analyzers available in the market. </p><p>Non-dispersive infrared analyzers (NDIR) use infrared light to detect and quantify specific gases in the exhaust. </p><p>Flame ionization detector (FID) analyzers measure total hydrocarbon emissions by burning the sample in a hydrogen flame and measuring the resulting ions. </p><p>Chemiluminescence analyzers (CLA) capture and measure the chemiluminescent light produced when certain gases react with ozone. </p><p>Constant volume sampler (CVS) collects a sample of the exhaust gases for subsequent analysis using other methods. </p><p>Other types of analyzers may include electrochemical sensors or gas chromatographs, which offer different approaches to exhaust gas analysis.</p></p>
