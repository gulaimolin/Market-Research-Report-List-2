<p><p>Parenteral dosage forms are pharmaceutical formulations that are administered through routes other than the gastrointestinal tract. The market types within parenteral dosage forms include emulsions for injection, powders for injection, and gels for injection. Emulsions for injection are oil-in-water or water-in-oil dispersions, commonly used for poorly water-soluble drugs. Powders for injection are dry mixtures that require reconstitution before administration, ensuring stability and ease of transport. Gels for injection are semi-solid formulations that provide sustained drug release and are used for localized applications or drug depot formulations.</p></p>
