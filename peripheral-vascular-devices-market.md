<p><h1>Peripheral Vascular Devices Market Growth, Market Trends, COVID-19 Impact, and Forecasts for period from 2024 - 2031</h1></p><p><strong>Peripheral Vascular Devices Market Analysis and Latest Trends</strong></p>
<p><p>Peripheral vascular devices are medical devices used in the treatment of peripheral artery diseases (PADs) that affect the blood vessels outside the heart and brain. These diseases often result in narrowing or blockage of the blood vessels, leading to reduced blood flow to the limbs. Peripheral vascular devices aid in restoring blood flow by removing or bypassing blockages and improving circulation.</p><p>The market for peripheral vascular devices is expected to grow at a CAGR of 8.8% during the forecast period. This growth can be attributed to several factors, including the rising prevalence of PADs due to lifestyle changes, increasing geriatric population, and advancements in technology. Additionally, the growing demand for minimally invasive procedures and the development of innovative products further contribute to the market expansion.</p><p>In recent years, there have been several notable trends in the peripheral vascular devices market. One such trend is the increasing adoption of drug-coated balloons (DCBs) and drug-eluting stents (DES) for the treatment of PADs. These devices release anti-restenotic drugs, reducing the risk of vessel re-narrowing and improving long-term patency rates.</p><p>Another significant trend is the rising demand for minimally invasive procedures, such as endovascular surgeries, as they offer advantages like shorter hospital stays, faster recovery times, and reduced trauma to the patient. Furthermore, advancements in imaging techniques have allowed for better visualization and navigation during procedures, leading to improved outcomes.</p><p>Moreover, the market is witnessing product innovations and technological advancements, such as the development of bioresorbable stents and the integration of robotic systems for precise interventions. These advancements are expected to drive market growth by improving treatment efficacy and patient outcomes.</p><p>Overall, the peripheral vascular devices market is projected to experience substantial growth, driven by factors like the increasing prevalence of PADs, technological advancements, and the adoption of minimally invasive procedures. The introduction of innovative products and the growing focus on improving patient care and outcomes are expected to further fuel market expansion in the coming years.</p></p>
