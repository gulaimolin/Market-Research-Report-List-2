<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>Aspherical mirrors find applications in various industries, with two popular market types being toroidal mirrors and off-axis parabolic mirrors. Toroidal mirrors have a non-spherical shape that allows them to focus light more precisely, reducing spherical and chromatic aberrations. They are often used in telescopes, microscopes, and imaging systems. On the other hand, off-axis parabolic mirrors have a parabolic shape but are not centered on the optical axis. This design allows for distortion-free imaging and is commonly used in satellite communications, astronomy, and laser systems.</p></p>
