<p><p>Clinical examination is a fundamental component of medical practice that involves systematically evaluating patients' physical signs, symptoms, medical history, and conducting various diagnostic tests to determine their overall health status. The market is driven by several factors, including the rising prevalence of chronic diseases, the increasing geriatric population, technological advancements in diagnostic devices, and the growing demand for non-invasive diagnostic procedures. Additionally, the COVID-19 pandemic has further highlighted the importance of clinical examination in early detection and containment of diseases.</p><p>One of the latest trends in the clinical examination market is the increasing adoption of point-of-care (POC) diagnostic tests. These tests provide rapid and accurate results at the patient's bedside, enabling timely diagnosis and treatment decisions. POC diagnostic devices are compact, user-friendly, and require minimal sample volumes, making them ideal for use in various healthcare settings such as hospitals, clinics, and home care.</p><p>Another trend is the integration of artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning (ML) in clinical examination practices.
