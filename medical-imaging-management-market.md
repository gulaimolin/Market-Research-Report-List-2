<p><p>The Medical Imaging Management market consists of two main types: the Vendor Neutral Archive System (VNA) and the Picture Archiving and Communications System (PACS). VNA is a centralized repository that enables healthcare facilities to store, manage, and share medical images and patient information from various sources without format restrictions. On the other hand, PACS is a comprehensive system that includes both image storage and communication capabilities, allowing healthcare providers to capture, store, distribute, and display medical images and related data. Both systems play crucial roles in facilitating efficient management and access to medical imaging information.</p></p>
