<p><p>Satellite propulsion systems are categorized into three types based on their propulsion mechanisms. Chemical propulsion satellites utilize chemical reactions to generate thrust, which allows for high performance but limited lifespan. Hybrid propulsion satellites combine chemical and electric propulsion, enhancing efficiency and extending mission durations. All-electric propulsion satellites solely rely on electric propulsion, achieving low thrust but significantly longer operational lifetimes. Each market type has its own advantages and limitations, influencing the choice of propulsion system based on the specific requirements of satellite missions.</p></p>
